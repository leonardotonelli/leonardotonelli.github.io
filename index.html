Research Case Studies

Selected Work

Deep Q-Network Reproduction on Atari

Reproduced the original DQN training logic on Demon Attack to study learning stability under limited compute.

Problem. Can value-based deep RL recover coherent behavior when strict computational limits force short training horizons?

Method. Implemented a CNN DQN in PyTorch and Gymnasium with experience replay, target networks, reward clipping, and epsilon-greedy exploration, following Mnih et al.

Result. Over 1,000 episodes (about 3 hours), reward and Q-value trajectories were noisy but increased, and frame-level high/low Q inspection confirmed non-random policy structure.

Why it matters. Established a reproducible RL pipeline and highlighted which stability components are most sensitive when resources are constrained.

Binary Perceptron Thesis: Entropy-Biased Search in NP-Hard Landscapes

Replicated and extended entropy-based algorithms for the binary perceptron using tools from statistical mechanics.

Problem. How can learning dynamics be steered toward dense solution regions in an NP-hard constraint satisfaction setting?

Method. Derived and implemented Replicated Simulated Annealing and Replicated Gradient Descent from large-deviation formulations with interacting replica Hamiltonians.

Result. Across problem sizes above 500 and constraint densities up to 0.5, replicated methods consistently improved convergence over non-interacting baselines and clarified phase-transition behavior.

Why it matters. This work connects abstract theory to algorithmic design and motivates future study of geometry-aware optimization in difficult learning landscapes.

Credit Scoring Under Severe Class Imbalance

Built and compared imbalance-aware classifiers for default prediction where positive events were only 6.7% of the data.

Problem. How do we optimize risk detection when standard classifiers are biased toward the majority class?

Method. Combined feature engineering, resampling, cost-sensitive splits, and ensemble methods (Balanced RF, Easy Ensemble, Balanced Bagging, boosted trees with random undersampling).

Result. Improved ROC-AUC from 0.8077 (logistic baseline) to 0.8582 with boosted trees plus undersampling, while maintaining stronger recall behavior on defaults.

Why it matters. Shows how statistical design choices in evaluation and sampling can materially change decisions in high-cost prediction settings.

Optimizer Benchmarking: ResNet and Vision Transformer

Compared Adam, Muon, Scion, and Dion on CIFAR-10 with a controlled mixed-precision experimental pipeline.

Problem. Which optimizer behaviors matter most across architecture classes: early convergence speed, stability, or final generalization?

Method. Trained each optimizer-architecture pair for 80 epochs in PyTorch with AMP and standardized logging, then analyzed loss and validation trajectories.

Result. Scion showed faster early convergence on ResNet with competitive final accuracy; Muon exposed a conditioning-versus-update-cost trade-off.

Why it matters. Reinforces that optimizer choice should be interpreted through architecture and compute budget, not aggregate leaderboard performance.

Dimensionality Reduction Benchmarking Framework

Developed a unified framework to compare manifold and representation methods across synthetic and real datasets.

Problem. How should we evaluate low-dimensional embeddings when local fidelity, global geometry, and downstream utility often conflict?

Method. Benchmarked PCA, Isomap, LLE, Laplacian Eigenmaps, t-SNE, UMAP, and autoencoders using Averaged Jaccard, random triplet accuracy, k-NN accuracy, ARI, and Procrustes StabAUC.

Result. Quantified the local-global trade-off (for example, stronger local structure for t-SNE and UMAP, stronger global ordering for PCA and Isomap) and documented large robustness differences across hyperparameter regimes.

Why it matters. Provides an evidence-oriented template for selecting embedding methods based on objective and stability requirements, not visual appeal alone.

Expectation-Maximization vs Modern Imputation

Compared EM, MICE, kNN, and simple baselines under controlled missingness mechanisms and real incomplete clinical data.

Problem. When does classical likelihood-based imputation remain preferable to modern flexible alternatives?

Method. Ran factorial simulations under MCAR, MAR, and MNAR; implemented EM for multivariate normal recovery and semi-supervised Gaussian mixtures from first principles.

Result. EM showed expected strength under MAR, while MNAR produced a non-zero asymptotic bias floor; runtime analysis clarified scalability trade-offs versus kNN and MICE.

Why it matters. Connects theoretical assumptions directly to empirical failure modes, improving method choice in incomplete-data workflows.
